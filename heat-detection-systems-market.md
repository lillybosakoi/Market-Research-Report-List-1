Heat detectors monitor temperature changes, ideal for environments with smoke interference. Ionization smoke detectors utilize radioactive materials to sense fast flaming fires, while photoelectric smoke detectors rely on light scattering to detect smoldering fires. Additionally, ionization/photoelectric smoke detectors combine both technologies, offering versatile detection for diverse fire scenarios. This market segment is vital for enhancing safety and compliance in residential, commercial, and industrial settings.</p></p>

<p><p>The RF and Microwave Ceramic Capacitors market is primarily divided into two categories: Class I and Class II capacitors. Class I ceramic capacitors are characterized by stable capacitance values and low loss, making them ideal for high-frequency applications. They typically use materials like NP0 (C0G) for superior temperature stability. Class II capacitors, such as X7R and Y5V, offer higher capacitance values but exhibit greater variation with temperature and voltage, making them suitable for applications where space is limited and performance is flexible.</p></p>
